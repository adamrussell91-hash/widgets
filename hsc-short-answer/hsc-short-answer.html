    Self-mark with the margin protocol

    This is your judgement, not the tool's. First annotate your handwritten essay, then report what the chains show and locate yourself against each checklist item.

    1 · Annotate the evidence chains in the margin

    △ Triangle — a piece of evidence (a quotation)
    □ Square — you state its effect
    ○ Circle — you link it to your topic sentence and the question

    A healthy paragraph repeats △ □ ○. A triangle with no square is evidence with no effect — quoting without analysing. The tool cannot see your essay; you must actually annotate it.

    How many chains are complete (△□○)?
    Which paragraphs break the chain, and how?

    2 · Locate yourself against each checklist item

    3 · Optional — confirm a quotation you used

    You wrote from memory. Paste a quotation to confirm it against the verified bank. The bank is a sample of key quotations, not the whole play — not found does not mean your quote is wrong. Ticking your checklist is always enough.
